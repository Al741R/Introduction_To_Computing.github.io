The Internet and the World Wide Web




What is the Internet?

As we all know, the advancement of todays technology enables the computer to connect to any other computer from around the Globe. The Internet Access in one of the major reasons business, home, and other users purchase computers. Through the Internet, society has access to global information and instant communications.

The internet is a worldwide system of computer networks. A network of networks in which users at any one computer can, if they have permission, get information from any other computer.



History of The Internet

The Internet started as a networking project by the U.S. Department of Defence.

The Advanced Research Projects Agency (ARPA) was commissioned to establish a secure network Iinking centers working on military research in such a way that the communications would remain intact even if some of the centers were knocked out.

The product of this project is called ARPANet and became functional in September 1969.


The Network was built with two main goal.

1. First, to allow scientists from different locations to share information and work together on military and scientific projects.

2. And could function even if part of the network were disabled or destroyed by a disaster such as a nuclear attack.

The original network consisted of four main computers located in:

1. University of California at Los Angeles

2. University of California at Santa Barbara

3. Stanford Research Institute

4. University of Utah


Each computer serves as a host. A host or server is any computer that provides services and connections to other computers on a network.




How The Internet Works

Data sent over the Internet travels via networks and communications media owned and operated by many companies.

So how does the internet works?

The Internet works through a packet routing network in accordance with the Internet Protocol (IP), the Transport Control Protocol (TCP) and other protocols. The IP Address will provide destination and source for the packet as it traverse through the network.



Terminologies

Data Packet - a unit of data made into a single package that travels along a given network path. They are use to transmit data that navigates a network.

Internet Protocol (IP)- a set of rules that dictate how data should be delivered over the public network.

Transmission Control Protocol (TCP) - a connection-oriented communications protocol that facilitates the exchange of messages between computing devices in a network.

IP address - is a number that uniquely identifies each computer or device connected to the Internet. (Example: 192.245.25.3). It allows computers to send and receive information, and Every device in a Network should have an IP Address.



Domain Name

The Domain Name is the address or the name of the website we enter to access them.

Domain Name System

It is a hierarchal naming system to better organize the Domain Name. The domain name system maps the name people use to locate a website to the IP address that a computer uses to locate a website.




Establish Internet Connection

Internet Service Provider (ISP)

An ISP is a company or organization that provides internet services for accessing , using, or participating in the Internet.



Modem

• It is short for "Modulator-Demodulator”


• This device receives the information from your ISP, enabling your device to connect to the Internet.


• It converts or "modulates" an analog signal from a telephone or cable wire to digital data (1s and 0s) that a computer can recognize.



Dial-up access

• The modem in your computer uses a standard telephone line to connect to the Internet.

• The parts of a dial-up connection include a modem, which transmits the signal from your computer to your phone line, and connects it to the modem at your ISP, as well as a telephone line.

• However, it has a slow speed.



How it Connects

1. The user will initiates a dial-up connection

2. The modem dials a phone number of an Internet Service Provider (ISP) that is designated to receive dial-up calls.

3. The ISP then establishes the connection,

4. The user will hear several beeping an buzzing sounds when the connection is established.

Aside from the slow connection, one of the major drawbacks is when you're using dial-up service, your landline is tied up.



Broadband Connection

The term broadband simply means a Broad band of frequencies has been used.

It is offered in different forms:

1. DSL

2. Cable

3. Fiber Optic

4. Wireless

5. Satellite



Digital Subscriber Line (DSL)

• The DSL (or Digital Subscriber Line) internet service makes its connection by utilizing unused telephone wires that cause no interruption to your telephone service.

• The DSL retain the capability of Dial up connection, but it automatically connects your device to the internet without any initiation.



Cable

• Cable modem service enables cable operators to provide broadband using the same coaxial cables that deliver pictures and sound to your TV set.

• Subscribers can access their cable modem service by simply turning on their computers, without dialing-up an ISP. You can still watch cable TV while using it.



Fiber-Optic

• An Internet connection that transfers data fully or partially via fiber optic cables.

• “Fiber” refers to the thin glass wires inside the larger protective cable. “Optic” means light, it refers to the way the type of data is transferred.



Wireless

• Wireless broadband connects a home or business to the Internet using a radio link between the customers location and the service provides facility.

• Wireless connection is either by WiFi(Wireless Fidelity) or by Cellular Network.

• Wi-Fi allows your devices to connect to the Internet via a router.

• Cellular data allows your devices to connect to the Internet via a cell phone signal.



Satellite Modem

Communicates with a satellite dish to provide high-speed Internet connections via satellite.

A good replacement for other types of connection for those people living in remote rural areas, however, the installation costs are quite high.




The Word Wide Web

The World Wide Web (WWW), or Web, is a widely used service on the Internet, consists of a worldwide collection of electronic documents. Each electronic document on the Web, is called a Webpage.

HISTORY

The World Wide Web was developed in 1989 by Tim Berners-Lee, a computer scientist at the European Organization for Nuclear Research (CERN) in Geneva, Switzerland. He also wrote the first browser in 1990.



Website

A Web site is a collection of related Web pages and associated items, such as documents and pictures, stored on a Web server.

A Web Server is a computer that delivers requested Web pages to your computer.



World Wide Web Coding Languages

• The part of a website that the user interacts with directly is termed the front end. It is also referred to as the ‘client side of the application.

• The backend is the server side of the website. It stores and arranges data, and also makes sure everything on the client side of the website works fine. It is the part of the website that you cannot see and interact with. It is the portion of software that does not come in direct contact with the users.
